Lactase: Structure and Function in the Digestive Tract

What is lactase?

Lactase is the enzyme made by the enterocytes that line the small intestine. Its job is to break apart lactose (the main disaccharide sugar in milk and other dairy products) into two smaller sugars that are easier for the body to absorb.

Why does it matter?

Did you know? Roughly two-thirds of the world's adult population has reduced lactase activity after childhood.
Did you know? A single base pair difference in a regulatory sequence on a gene that doesn't even code for lactase itself can determine whether an individual develops lactase non-persistence or lactase persistence, and subsequently, whether they are lactose intolerant or are able to properly digest lactose.
