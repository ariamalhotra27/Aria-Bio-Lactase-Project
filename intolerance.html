When Lactase Is Missing

Home/ When It's Missing

1. Lactose moves on

When there isn't enough lactase on the brush border, most to all of the lactose in a meal doesn't get hydrolyzed in the small intestine. Since it is therefore not broken down into smaller pieces, it cannot go through the small intestine into the bloodstream. Instead, it passes through the intestine without being absorbed, and enters the large intestine (specifically the colon) still as a disaccharide.
